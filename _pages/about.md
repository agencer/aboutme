I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at New York University, specializing in comparative politics and quantitative methods.

My research studies how citizens evaluate political information under complexity, uncertainty, and democratic backsliding. I focus on reform bundles, political accountability, out-group stereotypes, and the strategic presentation of institutional change. Methodologically, I use survey experiments, causal inference, computational social science, and formal theory.

My dissertation examines how complexity and selective presentation shape political judgment in backsliding democracies. My current projects study autocratic capture through bundled reforms, stereotype-prone political beliefs, and optimal allocation of compliance incentives in experimental research.
